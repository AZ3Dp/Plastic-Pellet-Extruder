# Introduction
Pellet extruders are becoming necessary because the way we think about 3D printing is evolving.
For years, filament has been the standard. It’s convenient and clean, but it’s also expensive, restrictive, and inefficient at scale. Every spool represents extra cost and extra limitations on material choice. Once you start thinking beyond hobby printing—into large-format parts, rapid prototyping, robotics, or production runs—you quickly feel those constraints.
Pellet extruders remove that bottleneck.
They let you print directly from raw plastic pellets, the same form manufacturers use. That means dramatically lower material costs, access to a wider range of engineering polymers, easier blending of additives, and the ability to use recycled material. For large parts especially, the savings aren’t just noticeable—they’re transformative.
There’s also a sustainability angle. Filament production adds another manufacturing step, more energy, and more packaging. Pellet systems shorten the supply chain and make closed-loop recycling far more practical.

# My project
I designed this pellet extruder to be a great baseline to be adapted for many printers. I have currently made it so that it can be mounted onto the ever-so-popular Prusa i3 MK3s. Simply connect the extruder to your 3D printer, and start feeding pellets!
